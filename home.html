WEB DESIGN

Web design is the design of webpages displayed in browsers. In the past, web design was solely focused on desktop browser, however in recent years, "design for mobile and tablet browsers has become ever-increasingly important."1

Web design can refer to "appearance, layout, and, in some cases, content of a website."1 The appearance of a webpage refers to the colors, fonts, sizing, images, and videos that are displayed on the page. The layout of a webpage refers to how content is structured. A good website is easy to use. As a matter of fact, "many webpages are designed with a focus on simplicity, so that no extraneous information and functionality that might distract or confuse users appears."1

Webpages may contain functional elements, which are imperative in "giving your users the best possible experience."2 Some of these elements include "navigation, user interactions, animations, speed, site structure, and cross-browser and cross-device compatibility."2 Not all of them have to be implemented, but the easier a website is to use, the better.
